Benefits by State

Michigan

Print-and-take checklist. Tick each box as you go, and keep it with your paperwork.

  • Download your VA letter showing a 100% permanent and total rating, Individual Unemployability paid at the 100% rate, or a Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) certificate, then come back to Rated, Now What.
  • Confirm you qualify through one of Michigan's three routes (MCL 211.7b), since there is no partial tier below 100%, no income limit, no value cap, and no separate blindness or limb-loss category.
  • Download Form 5107 (the property tax exemption affidavit), then come back to Rated, Now What.
  • Fill out Form 5107 and file it with your city or township assessor before the March Board of Review.
  • If you miss the March Board of Review, ask your assessor about the July or December Board of Review as a fallback.
  • Note that once approved (as of January 1, 2025), you no longer have to refile this exemption every year.
  • Bring your VA letter to a Secretary of State branch (find one with the SOS office locator) for the vehicle plate, and confirm the current DV plate fees at the branch.
  • Do not plan around HB 5274 (the vehicle sales-tax exemption): it is still in committee, tied to HB 5257, and not yet law.
  • Route any VA rating, appeal, or Individual Unemployability question to a free VA-accredited Veterans Service Officer (VSO), never a paid company.
