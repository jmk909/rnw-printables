Benefits by State

Pennsylvania

Print-and-take checklist. Tick each box as you go, and keep it with your paperwork.

  • Download your VA letter at VA.gov showing a 100% schedular rating, Individual Unemployability paid at the 100% rate, or statutory blindness, paraplegia, or loss of two or more limbs, then come back to Rated, Now What.
  • Know that Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) is not a recognized Pennsylvania pathway, so qualify through one of the routes above.
  • Confirm your household income is under the $114,637 limit (effective January 1, 2025); note some county forms still print the older $95,279 figure.
  • Check the ownership rule (51 Pa. C.S. 8902(3): sole, joint with spouse, or estate by the entireties) for your principal dwelling.
  • Find your County Director of Veterans Affairs using the County Director locator, then come back to Rated, Now What.
  • File MA-VA Form 40 with your County Director for the real estate tax exemption (surviving spouses use MA-VA Form 40ss).
  • Use VA Form 3288 if you need to request supporting records from the VA.
  • File PennDOT Form MV-145V for the vehicle plate benefit.
  • Expect a review of your exemption roughly every 5 years, and do not wait on HB 1257 (a constitutional amendment that needs two sessions plus a referendum and is not yet law).
  • Route any VA rating, appeal, or Individual Unemployability question to a free VA-accredited Veterans Service Officer (VSO), never a paid company.
