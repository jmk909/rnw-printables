Benefits by State

Virginia

Print-and-take checklist. Tick each box as you go, and keep it with your paperwork.

  • Download your VA letter at VA.gov showing a 100% schedular permanent and total rating or Individual Unemployability paid at the 100% rate (both are written into Va. Code 58.1-3219.5), then come back to Rated, Now What.
  • Know that Specially Adapted Housing, blindness, limb loss, and paraplegia are not separate Virginia pathways; eligibility is driven by your rating.
  • Apply for the property tax exemption through your local government (there is no income or asset limit, it covers up to one acre, there is no statewide deadline, and localities respond within about 20 business days).
  • If your rating is below 100%, ask your locality about its local means-tested relief instead.
  • If you are a surviving spouse, confirm you qualify (veteran's death on or after January 1, 2011, no remarriage; the benefit is portable if you move).
  • Download Form VSA 54 for your disabled veteran license plate (then come back to Rated, Now What) and file it with the DMV.
  • Download the Virginia State Parks passport form to apply for your parks passport, then come back to Rated, Now What.
  • Apply for your discounted lifetime hunting and fishing license through the Department of Wildlife Resources (DWR) using Form OUT-010; fees are per license type ($0 fishing, $0 hunting, $15 trapping if totally and permanently disabled; $50 each at 70% or more; $75 each at 50 to under 70%; $100 each at 30 to under 50%; optional $10 hard card).
  • If you are a nonresident, confirm the DWR annual disabled-veteran license fee and terms at dwr.virginia.gov/veterans, since that figure is not stated here.
  • Route any VA rating, appeal, or Individual Unemployability question to a free VA-accredited Veterans Service Officer (VSO), never a paid company.
