Benefits by State

North Carolina

Print-and-take checklist. Tick each box as you go, and keep it with your paperwork.

  • Download your VA letter at VA.gov showing a 100% permanent and total rating, Individual Unemployability certified permanent and total, or a Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) certification under 38 U.S.C. 2101, then come back to Rated, Now What.
  • Gather your DD-214 showing honorable discharge and proof you owned the home as of January 1.
  • File Form NCDVA-9 and Form AV-9 with your county for the flat $45,000 property tax exclusion (no income limit) by the June 1 deadline, which is accepted through June 1 preceding the tax year.
  • If you do not meet the disabled-veteran threshold, ask about the separate income-limited Elderly or Disabled exclusion on the same Form AV-9.
  • Do not count on Senate Bill 660 (the Honoring Sacrifice / NC Veterans Relief Act): it is not yet law, so its proposed higher figures do not apply.
  • Claim your income tax items on Form D-400 Schedule S (North Carolina's flat individual rate is 3.99% for 2026).
  • File Form MVR-33A with the DMV for the vehicle benefit.
  • Buy your discounted State Parks pass ($90 or $150 as listed) and get your hunting and fishing license through the Wildlife Resources Commission (confirm license fees on its fee schedule).
  • Find your nearest NC DMVA county service office for free help using the DMVA county service office locator.
  • Route any VA rating, appeal, or Individual Unemployability question to a free VA-accredited Veterans Service Officer (VSO), never a paid company, and report scams to the VA Office of Inspector General hotline.
